# Badge System

## Badge groups

### A. Assessment Mastery Badges

Assessment badges use literal, student-friendly labels. They are retained for the full year and can upgrade Bronze → Silver → Gold.

| Badge | Main evidence source |
|---|---|
| MCQ Mastery Badge | Qualifying MCQ challenges and unit-length sets |
| HIPP Mastery Badge | Source-analysis practice and applied sourcing |
| SAQ Mastery Badge | Short-answer practice and assessments |
| DBQ Mastery Badge | DBQ practice, full DBQs, and revisions |
| LEQ Mastery Badge | LEQ practice, full LEQs, and revisions |
| Vocabulary Mastery Badge | Vocabulary challenge performance |
| Timeline Mastery Badge | Chronology / causation / sequence tasks |
| Revision Mastery Badge | Verified feedback-and-revision cycles |

### B. Unit Collection Badges

Unit badges are one-time, thematic achievements. They reward content exploration and build toward a Unit Master Seal. They are not three-tiered by default.

## Badge states

| State | Visual treatment | Student message |
|---|---|---|
| Locked | Grayscale silhouette, low opacity | “Complete your first qualifying activity to begin.” |
| Available | Full-color outline, 0% bar | “Ready to begin.” |
| Active | Full color, visible progress ring / bar | “Silver progress: 2 of 4 qualifying tasks.” |
| Bronze / Silver | Tier material or accent changes, next tier shown | “Next target is visible.” |
| Gold | Full-color gold seal, completed ribbon | “Mastery complete.” |
| Earned Unit Badge | Full color in collection case | “Added to Unit 1 collection.” |

## General badge default tiers

Teachers should be able to edit thresholds, but the starter thresholds are intentionally clear.

| Badge | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---|---|---|---|
| MCQ Mastery | 1 qualifying set at 60%+ | 3 qualifying sets at 70%+ | 85%+ on a unit-length or cumulative set, or teacher-approved equivalent |
| HIPP Mastery | 2 source analyses meeting 3/4 criteria | 5 source analyses meeting 3/4 criteria | Apply sourcing effectively in a DBQ or equivalent assessed writing |
| SAQ Mastery | 2/3 on one SAQ | 3/3 on two SAQs | 3/3 on three SAQs across different units, or a teacher-approved revision pathway |
| DBQ Mastery | 3/7 or higher on a complete DBQ | 5/7 or higher | 6/7 or higher, or revise a 5/7 to 6/7 |
| LEQ Mastery | 3/6 or higher | 4/6 or higher | 5/6 or higher, or teacher-approved revision pathway |
| Vocabulary Mastery | 2 challenge sets at 70%+ | 4 challenge sets at 80%+ | 6 challenge sets at 90%+ |
| Timeline Mastery | 2 complete tasks | 4 tasks at 80%+ | 6 tasks at 90%+ or a teacher-approved synthesis task |
| Revision Mastery | 1 verified revision | Improve one rubric point on 2 tasks | Improve two points on a major writing task plus a reflection |

## Badge progress should be explainable

Every badge card must answer:

```text
What counts toward this badge?
What have I completed?
What is my next target?
What rewards come with the next tier?
```

Example:

```text
MCQ MASTERY BADGE — SILVER IN PROGRESS

Progress: 2 / 3 qualifying 70%+ MCQ sets
Next reward: 60 Historian XP + 8 Archive Tokens
```

Do not hide badge criteria behind tooltips only. Students need to be able to self-monitor.

## Completion rules

- A completed tier stays earned forever.
- Badge tiers should be awarded once and recorded in a tier ledger.
- A quest can contribute to multiple badges, but avoid linking more than two assessment badges plus one Unit badge to a single small task.
- Store items can celebrate a badge, but they may not alter graded assessment conditions.
