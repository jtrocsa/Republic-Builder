# Unit 1 Primary-Source Library

## Source selection principles

The archive prioritizes sources that are:

- directly connected to Unit 1’s required developments;
- usable for sourcing, comparison, causation, and contextualization;
- available from an archival, library, public-domain, or established educational repository;
- documented with a citation and source-access link;
- suitable for short classroom excerpts, visual analysis, or a document set.

The archive does **not** bundle external scans. Before placing a scanned image, a modern translation, or a transcript in a public GitHub deployment, verify its rights statement at the individual repository level.

## Strong Unit 1 DBQ document combination

The demo’s seven-document packet uses:

1. Waldseemüller map
2. Columbus letter
3. *El Requerimiento*
4. Cortés letter
5. Florentine Codex smallpox account
6. Las Casas account
7. Harriot report

Suggested outside evidence options for teacher scoring include:

- encomienda;
- the New Laws;
- the transatlantic slave trade;
- the conquest of the Inca Empire;
- Spanish missions;
- the Pueblo Revolt should **not** be used for Unit 1 because it occurred in 1680, after the prompt’s time period.

## Recommended source-use pattern

Do not introduce a source only once. Reuse it across HIPP, multiple-choice, vocabulary, and writing.

Example: Columbus’s 1493 letter appears in a HIPP Challenge, an Evidence Royale set, the Unit Archive, and the DBQ packet. This helps students experience documents as evidence that can be interpreted differently depending on the task.
